Project Overview

A compact narrative summary of the analysis and findings.

This project examines how the 2019 Washington Nationals adjusted controllable batting decisions over the season, contributing to their historic comeback from a 19–31 start to winning the World Series.

Using MLB Statcast data, the analysis focuses on temporal shifts in batting strategies that players can actively control.

  • Spatial-temporal visualization of batted ball locations and spray angles
  • Analysis of launch metrics and pitch-type performance
  • Interactive dashboards built with Shiny to track strategic adjustments

Research Focus

The study investigates how changes in directional control, plate discipline, and launch quality evolved over time, particularly under high-leverage game situations.

Key Findings

  • Restored ability to drive the ball to all field directions as the season progressed
  • Increased run expectancy from opposite-field hard hits in late-season and postseason games
  • Improved plate discipline in high-leverage counts (e.g., 2–2, 3–1)
  • Significant improvement in weighted on-base average (wOBA) against horizontal breaking balls, indicating reduced directional constraint in batting decisions
